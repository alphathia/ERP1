## Task 3.1: Final Dimension Table (Iteration 2)

| Dim ID | Dimension Name | Hardened Definition | Value Creation Logic | Source Basis |
|--------|----------------|---------------------|---------------------|--------------|
| **D1** | Reasoning Sophistication | The **internal cognitive processes** an individual agent employs to analyze information, generate solutions, and refine outputs—**independent of whether other agents or humans are involved**. Captures *within-agent* cognition only. | Higher reasoning sophistication enables better decision quality, more innovative solutions, and successful handling of complex tasks | Sun et al. (2025); Liu et al. (2025); Li et al. (2025); Hatalis et al. (2023) |
| **D2** | Tool Integration Depth | The extent to which an agent can discover, select, invoke, and orchestrate external tools and services to accomplish tasks | Deeper tool integration expands agent action space, enables automation of technical tasks, and connects to enterprise systems for productivity and revenue value | Mohammadi et al. (2025); Liu et al. (2025); Zhang et al. (2026) |
| **D3** | Memory Persistence | The temporal scope and sophistication of information retention mechanisms that enable agents to maintain context and learn from experience | Greater memory persistence enables consistency over long interactions, personalization, and cumulative learning—critical for productivity and decision quality in extended engagements | Hatalis et al. (2023); Mohammadi et al. (2025) |
| **D4** | Coordination Paradigm | The **structural arrangement** by which multiple agents (or agent-human combinations) organize to divide labor, share information, and integrate outputs—**independent of any individual agent's reasoning capability**. Captures *between-agent architecture* only. | Multi-agent coordination enables division of labor, collective intelligence, and handling of tasks beyond single-agent capability—driving productivity and innovation at scale | Liu et al. (2025); Maldonado et al. (2024); Sun et al. (2025) |
| **D5** | Autonomy Level | The **locus of decision authority** determining what goals the agent pursues and what actions it takes—**specifically, the degree to which humans vs. the agent controls goal-setting and action-selection**. Captures *human-agent authority distribution* only. | Higher autonomy enables continuous operation and reduced human overhead, while managed autonomy balances productivity gains with appropriate risk mitigation | Morris et al. (2024); Acharya et al. (2025); Liu et al. (2025) |
| **D6** | Adaptation Mechanism | The means by which an agent improves its performance or adjusts to new situations over time | Effective adaptation reduces ongoing costs (no retraining), enables continuous improvement, and supports innovation through knowledge transfer | Li et al. (2025); Sun et al. (2025); Wu et al. (2024) |
| **D7** | Human Collaboration Mode | The nature and depth of interaction patterns between the agent and human users or team members | Appropriate collaboration modes build trust, ensure value alignment, and enable effective human-AI teaming—critical mediators of actual value realization | Wachowiak et al. (2024); Iftikhar et al. (2024); He et al. (2025) |
| **D8** | Compliance & Alignment Posture | The degree to which an agent operates within organizational policies, regulatory requirements, ethical standards, and intended value alignment | Robust compliance enables enterprise deployment by ensuring regulatory adherence, policy conformance, and sustained alignment with organizational values | OpenAI Preparedness Framework; Mohammadi et al. (2025); Liu et al. (2025); Acharya et al. (2025) |
| **D9** | Safety Architecture | The comprehensiveness of mechanisms protecting against harmful outputs, adversarial attacks, system failures, and unintended consequences | Comprehensive safety architecture is prerequisite for scaled deployment, protecting against liability, operational disruption, and reputational harm | OpenAI Preparedness Framework; Mohammadi et al. (2025); Piccialli et al. (2025) |

## Iteration 2 Decision

### ☑ **TERMINATE — All Ending Conditions Substantially Met**

**Rationale**:

1. **Objective conditions**: 6 of 8 met. The two "Not Met" conditions (no new dimensions, no splits) are **expected for Iteration 2** given the approved structural change (D8→D8+D9). These are process conditions that indicate *change occurred*, not quality failures.

2. **Subjective conditions**: All 5 met. The taxonomy is concise (9 dimensions), robust (25 unique profiles), comprehensive (all objects classified), extendible (Object Type annotations handle variety), and explanatory (value expectations mapped).

3. **Structural stability achieved**: 
   - No further dimension changes required
   - No characteristic boundary ambiguities identified
   - All objects classifiable without forcing
   - Value linkages comprehensive and validated

4. **Meta-characteristic alignment confirmed**: Every dimension and characteristic demonstrably relates to "the capacity of an agentic AI system to generate measurable organizational value"
